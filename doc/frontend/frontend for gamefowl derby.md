GAMEFOWL DERBY – GAME DESIGN PROGRESS
- Home Screen (Pre-Game Landing Page)
The opening screen greets the player with a bold presentation that establishes the competitive
theme.
Two beautifully rendered gamefowl roosters stand facing one another in the center of the screen.
They are proud and alert, with detailed feathers and vibrant colors. Subtle idle animations bring
them to life with slight feather movement and occasional head turns.
Above them appears the title:

- GAMEFOWL DERBY
The lettering is bold and stylized, similar to classic championship or derby branding with
metallic accents.
The background resembles a traditional derby arena environment with wooden rails, dusty
ground, and distant crowd ambience.

- Main Menu Options
Centered below the roosters are the main menu buttons.
Enter Tournament
Begins the main gameplay experience.
My Entry
Opens the player&#39;s selection of 20 breeds. Each breed includes an image and a short description.
The player may select up to 10 roosters of the same breed or mixed breeds.
The Keep
Allows players to choose between Bench and Flypen Keeps. Each breed responds differently to
the two conditioning styles.
Leaderboards
Displays top players, top breeds, and seasonal rankings.
Settings

The buttons are large, polished, and slightly animated, glowing softly when hovered over or
tapped.

- Ambient Details
To make the screen feel alive and immersive:
A faint crowd murmur plays in the background.
Occasionally one of the roosters gives a soft crow or wing rustle. No harsh or annoying sounds
are used.
Subtle camera movement slowly shifts perspective for a cinematic feel.
Light rays from above move gently across the scene.

- Derby Types
There are four types of derbies available to enter.
Long Heel
The favorite of the South.
Short Heel
The old timer&#39;s favorite.
Philipino
Fast and furious.
Mexican
Powerful but smart.
Each derby panel includes:
• A symbolic icon representing the style
• The derby name and short description
• Entry fee in coins
• Prize tier (Standard, Grand, Prestigious)
Prestigious derbies have higher entry costs but larger rewards.

- Selecting the Entry
After choosing the derby type, the player moves to the Entry Selection Screen.
A scrollable breed gallery displays the available breeds.
Each breed card contains:
• Breed name
• Large rooster image
• Short breed description
• Ratings based on five qualities

- Five Breed Qualities
1. Power
2. Speed
3. Intelligence
4. Accuracy
5. Stamina

- Example Breed Display
Redquill (in a Philipino Derby)
Power: Average
Speed: High
Intelligence: Medium
Accuracy: High
Stamina: Low
Players can select up to 10 roosters for their derby entry.
Each selected bird appears in the Entry Lineup Bar at the bottom of the screen.

Selecting the Keep
Once the entry lineup is chosen, the player selects the Keep style.

Bench Keep
A traditional conditioning style that improves:
• Endurance
• Durability
• Recovery
However, it slightly reduces explosive speed.
Flypen Keep
A more active conditioning environment that improves:
• Agility
• Speed
• Reflex
However, it slightly reduces stamina.

- Dynamic Attribute Adjustment
Once the player confirms their Keep and Derby type, the game engine automatically adjusts each
rooster&#39;s qualities.
The adjustment considers:
• The five breed qualities
• Derby style
• Keep type
Attribute bars visibly shift on the screen so the player can see how their birds change before
entering the derby.

- Derby Entry Confirmation
A confirmation panel appears showing:
Derby Name (example: The Claymore)
Derby Type (selected derby)
Entry Fee in coins

Roosters Entered (X / 10)
Keep Type (Bench or Flypen)
Buttons:
Enter the Derby
Return to Selection
When confirmed, coins are deducted and the entry is locked.

- Tournament Beginning
The screen fades into the arena.
Crowd noise gradually rises.
Arena lights brighten as the camera slowly moves across the ring.
Two entry banners appear on opposite sides.
Left Side: Player Entry Name
Right Side: Opponent Entry Name

- Announcer Introduction
The arena becomes briefly quiet.
The announcer speaks while the same words appear in stylized script on the screen.
Example:
&quot;Firebird entry… and Blueman entry!&quot;
The crowd then swells again as the match prepares to begin.
The camera slowly zooms toward the arena floor.

- The Match

The match screen transitions into the simulation interface.
The performance of each rooster is determined by:
• Breed qualities
• Keep effects
• Derby type
• Random competitive factors
The two birds move toward each other across the arena floor.
They rush toward each other and rise breast to breast with loud wing flapping.
The screen quickly fades to a bright red result screen.
Example result text:
Firebird&#39;s Dom scores a stunning one pitting win against Blueman&#39;s Blue!

- Experience Goal
Matches should feel:
• Dramatic
• Competitive
• Fast paced
The presentation should resemble a championship sporting broadcast.
